◈ FILE TS-A1 — THE DOCTRINE

We cannot secure what we cannot see.

America’s strategic supply chains run through adversary territory, through politically unstable regions, and through layers of intermediaries designed to obscure origin. The drugs in our hospitals, the components in our weapons systems, the minerals in our power grid, the medical supplies in our stockpile — sourced through chains optimized for cost, exploited by competitors, and invisible to the agencies responsible for securing them.

TheTradeSpy.ai closes that visibility gap. It is an autonomous intelligence platform that continuously verifies the true origin, ownership, and risk profile of products in America’s most critical supply chains — and answers the question procurement officers, policymakers, and national security leaders need answered:

Where is this actually made — and can we prove it?

The same architecture that exposes adversary control verifies domestic origin. For every product flagged as foreign-exposed, TheTradeSpy identifies the American-made alternatives — with evidence, with confidence tiers, and with the audit trail required to procure them under Buy American authority.

"The same engine that exposes hidden foreign exposure also identifies, verifies, and ranks American-made products eligible for federal purchase under Buy American and Trade Agreements Act authority. Adversary risk reduction and domestic procurement enablement are two outputs of one system."

The CCP hard floor is enforced in code — not in an AI prompt. No quality certification, no domestic manufacturing bonus, no human override can pull a flagged product below HIGH risk. That is not policy. That is architecture.

This is a production intelligence platform built by operators who lived the supply chain crisis firsthand. What follows is the case file.

THE STRATEGIC FRAME

Supply Chain Security is National Security.

This is no longer a procurement problem with national security consequences. It is a national security problem with procurement consequences.

Country-of-origin labels reflect final assembly, not actual manufacture. Sanctions lists capture named entities, not institutional architectures. Self-attestation databases are easily gamed. Periodic reports are obsolete the day they are published.

The agencies with the authority to act lack the tools to see — in either direction. They cannot reliably identify the products that should be flagged, and they cannot reliably verify the products that should be procured.

TheTradeSpy was built to provide that visibility — continuously, at scale, and with an evidentiary discipline that holds up to oversight.

Three Layers.
One System.

01
Strategy
Large language models identify the products that matter, define the evidence required to verify them, and design the specifications for the field agents that will collect that evidence. This is creative and analytical work — the kind of work language models do well. The output is a surveillance specification, not a risk determination.
LLM · STRATEGY · DESIGN
02
Engineering
Agent specifications are reviewed, hardened, and deployed by iRemedy engineers. No agent is created, modified, or released into production without human authorization. The platform is designed to scale; the deployment decision is not automated. Human authorization at every deployment gate — by design, not by policy.
HUMAN AUTH · ENGINEERING · DEPLOYMENT
03
Operation
Once deployed, agents run continuously. Field Agents collect evidence from federal and commercial sources. Scoring Agents apply a deterministic 63-factor model to convert that evidence into auditable risk scores. An independent Reconciler validates every conclusion. A Data Inspector flags discrepancies, stale data, and scoring drift. The result: tens of thousands of agents, deployed under human supervision, surveilling the supply chains the country depends on.
