◈ THE DATA GAP

What FDA Records Don't Tell You

FDA labeler records identify the U.S. marketing entity that filed the application. They do not identify where the drug was manufactured. A product labeled as a Pfizer product, headquartered in New York, may have its active pharmaceutical ingredient manufactured in Wuhan.

No government system resolves this at scale. No major distributor has built the infrastructure to resolve it. The gap has existed for decades.

iRemedy's Metapedia platform resolves it — cross-referencing DailyMed SPL data, openFDA records, customs bill-of-lading data, and FDA facility records to produce a composite confidence score for actual manufacturing origin on 2,009+ drug records and counting.

◈ THE INCENTIVE PROBLEM

Why GPOs Are Structurally Broken

Group Purchasing Organizations — the entities responsible for negotiating drug prices for hospitals — operate on a percentage-of-spend fee structure. They earn more when hospitals spend more.

This creates a structural disincentive to lower prices that is rarely articulated and almost never challenged in mainstream policy discussions. iRemedy developed this argument independently from first-principles analysis of GPO economics and presented it formally to CMS.

iRemedy's Safeway distribution infrastructure bypasses GPO lock-in entirely — offering transparent, direct wholesale pricing to 503A compounding pharmacies, independent pharmacies, and healthcare providers who choose not to be held captive by percentage-based contract structures.

◈ THE PLATFORM THESIS

Why Integration Changes Everything

Healthcare cost inflation is structurally driven by fragmentation. The distributor doesn't speak to the technology layer. The technology layer doesn't enforce compliance. Compliance is bolted on after the fact and generates friction at every step.

iRemedy was built on the thesis that integrating these three layers — licensed distribution, patented procurement technology, and compliance infrastructure — would produce something that none of the layers produces independently: a supply chain that is simultaneously efficient, transparent, and defensible.

Eighteen years of operations, six granted U.S. patents, and federal contracts totaling billions of dollars suggest the thesis is correct.
